Hub & Spoke Four Tiers Factory Model

Hub-and-Spoke organizational model

The Hub (T-Tech CoE) provides secure infrastructure and certified building blocks. The Spokes (divisions/regions) deliver context-specific solutions using those guardrails.

The Hub (T-Tech CoE)

  • Governance guardrails: DLP policies, environment strategy, security standards
  • Platform product: GaC/CaC catalog (certified connectors, templates, evidence automation)
  • Strategic delivery: SWAT teams for high-impact, cross-divisional projects
  • Academy: training curriculum and community building
  • Licensing & usage control: centralized Power Platform licenses and AI credits
  • Project approval & prioritization: review and prioritize projects based on value, risk, and resource capacity
  • Value measurement: measure value created by the platform (ROI, efficiency gains, cost savings)
  • P&L responsibility: financial accountability for platform run costs and EBIT targets

The Spokes (Divisions/Regions)

  • Enablement/Factory delivery: citizen developers and business technologists using Hub guardrails
  • Requirement gathering: identify high-value use cases and funnel complex ones to the Hub
  • Local adoption: first-level support and "how-to" guidance for makers
  • Context-specific solutions: adapt global templates to local regulations and business needs
  • Alliances & champions: division/spoke level alliances and champions to drive adoption and best practices
  • Special agreements: division/spoke level special agreements above standard tiers when needed (e.g., custom SLAs, dedicated capacity)

Why this works: Instead of breaking down silos by force, we empower them. The Hub provides "guarded highways" (secure infrastructure); the Spokes drive the cars (deliver solutions). Compliance is baked into platform templates, so local apps are "compliant by design."

Tiered delivery model

Platform & Governance = the foundation. Enablement = enablement & guardrails. Factory = factory execution. Strategic = SWAT teams.

Risk and value proportional governance

Governance scales with both risk and value. Using Golden Paths = fast approval. Custom work = manual review based on risk level and business value. This removes the "police bottleneck": teams choose Golden Paths because it's the fastest route to production.

Work item creation and routing workflow

1

Work item creation

Who creates: Business users, citizen developers, or business technologists submit automation requests through the platform portal.

Initial assessment: Each work item is evaluated for:

  • Value: Business impact, ROI potential, strategic alignment (cross-divisional, mission-critical, efficiency gains)
  • Risk: Data sensitivity, compliance requirements (TISAX, GDPR), integration complexity, system criticality
  • Scope: Personal productivity, team workflow, or enterprise-wide process

Routing decision: Based on value and risk assessment, work items are automatically routed to the appropriate tier (Enablement, Factory, or Strategic).

2

Golden Path compliance check

Automated scan: The platform automatically checks if the work item uses only pre-certified Golden Paths (certified connectors, templates, and components from the GaC/CaC catalog).

AI-powered compliance: Golden Path compliance checking can be automated via RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) and special AI workflows that analyze work item specifications against the certified component catalog, ensuring accurate routing and compliance validation.

Decision point:

✓ YES → Auto-approved (≈24 hours) → Enablement/Factory tier deployment
✗ NO → Manual review required → Risk and value assessment

Value proposition: Teams choose Golden Paths because they're faster (24-hour approval) rather than being forced. Custom logic triggers 6-week manual review, creating natural incentive alignment.

3

Manual review and risk/value assessment

When work items require custom logic or deviate from Golden Paths, they enter a manual review process that evaluates both risk and value. High risk items and Strategic tier activities are always custom projects requiring specialized delivery.

Factory tier backlog scheduling

For Factory tier work items (custom components and mini projects), the Step 3 decision determines placement in the public BPA backlog:

  • Work items are prioritized based on value, risk level, and business impact
  • Custom components and mini projects are scheduled for delivery by the factory (offshore/onshore hybrid model)
  • The backlog is publicly visible to all divisions, providing transparency on delivery capacity and timelines
  • Delivery estimates are provided based on backlog position, resource availability, and project complexity
Public backlog transparency

BPA maintains a public backlog of Factory and Strategic tier projects with delivery estimates:

  • Factory tier backlog: Custom components, mini projects, and standard process automation work items with estimated delivery dates
  • Strategic tier backlog: Strategic enterprise projects (always custom) with delivery timelines and resource allocation
  • Visibility: All divisions can view the backlog, understand capacity, and see when their work items are scheduled
  • Delivery estimates: Based on backlog position, project complexity, and factory capacity (onshore/offshore mix)
  • Prioritization: High-value, high-impact items are prioritized, but transparency ensures fair allocation across divisions
4

Approval and production deployment

Enablement tier approval
  • • Automated compliance checks run
  • • Evidence packs generated from logs/controls
  • • Human sign-off required for production promotion
  • • DLP policy compliance verified
  • • Connector usage audit completed
  • • Timeline: 24 hours (auto-approval) or 1 week (manual review)
Factory tier approval
  • • Code review by Munich architects
  • • Automated compliance evidence generation
  • • ALM pipeline validation (Dev/Test/Prod)
  • • Quality gates: Golden Paths + review + automated checks
  • • Production promotion triggers final review
  • • Timeline: 1-2 weeks (standard review)
Strategic tier approval
  • • Enterprise-grade code review
  • • Security assessment by T-Tech security team
  • • Penetration testing required
  • • CISO sign-off mandatory
  • • Worker Council approval (if applicable)
  • • TISAX/GDPR pre-certification
  • • Timeline: 4 weeks + security sign-off

Key governance principles

  • Compliance by design: Golden Paths are pre-certified, making compliance automatic rather than a gate
  • Value-driven routing: High-value opportunities (cross-divisional, strategic) are prioritized and routed to appropriate tiers
  • Risk-proportional review: Review depth and duration scale with risk level, not uniformly applied
  • Speed through standards: Teams are incentivized to use Golden Paths because it's faster, not because it's mandated
  • Evidence automation: Audit trails and compliance evidence are generated automatically, reducing manual overhead

Multi-location delivery capability

T-Tech can leverage multiple locations within the TÜV SÜD global footprint to optimize cost and delivery efficiency:

  • Onshore (Munich, Germany): Front Office functions—Solution Architects, Engagement Managers, Business Analysts. High-touch, German-speaking, culturally aligned with HQ. Handles architecture, stakeholder management, risk/security alignment, and political complexities. TÜV SÜD's headquarters and primary operations center in Germany [TÜV SÜD About Us]
  • Offshore (Pune, India & Hungary): Back Office factory—Developers, QA Engineers, Support Ops. TÜV SÜD operates globally with facilities in over 50 countries, including significant presence in India and Eastern Europe. These locations serve as production assets, leveraging existing legal entities to optimize delivery costs [TÜV SÜD Global Presence]. India and Eastern Europe are established offshore development hubs with proven cost advantages [Gartner: Offshore Development Centers]
  • China (21Vianet): Separate tenant strategy for data residency compliance. Federated architecture with same policy templates as global tenant. China's Cybersecurity Law (CSL) and Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL) require data residency for Chinese operations [TÜV SÜD Asia Pacific]
Cost optimization impact
  • Labor arbitrage: Shifting execution work to offshore locations reduces blended cost rate by 40-50% through geographic cost differentials. Industry studies show typical savings of 30-60% for offshore development work [McKinsey: Future of Offshoring]. Example: 100-hour Factory Tier app costs €10,000 in Munich vs. €3,000 in offshore locations
  • Typical mix: 80% offshore (execution, testing, standard workflows) + 20% onshore (architecture, review, risk management) achieves optimal cost/quality balance. This hybrid model is standard practice in global IT delivery [Accenture: Offshore Development]
  • Quality protection: Offshore teams use Certified Modules and follow Golden Paths. Mandatory code review by onshore architects ensures quality. CI/CD pipeline runs automated compliance checks. Quality frameworks and governance models mitigate risks in distributed delivery [PMI: Distributed Teams Quality]
Application across tiers
  • Platform & Governance: Hub manages multi-location delivery strategy, quality gates, and resource allocation across locations
  • Enablement: Primarily in-house (Munich) for enablement and support
  • Factory: Hybrid-shore model (80/20 offshore/onshore) for cost-efficient factory execution
  • Strategic: Hybrid-shore model (70/30 offshore/onshore) for premium delivery with cost optimization
