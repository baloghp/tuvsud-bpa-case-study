Anticipated Q&A

On "Token Burning" & AI Cost Control

The Hard Question:

"We have people spinning up agents and burning AI credits with no ROI. How do you stop the bleeding without killing innovation?"

The Answer:

"We solve this with Environment Routing, not bans. I give makers a 'Sandbox' environment where innovation is free but usage is capped (no real cost). To move to 'Production' (where costs accrue), they must submit a business case. Furthermore, by shifting to a Retainer/Chargeback model, the Divisions see these costs on their own P&L. When they pay the bill, they self-regulate immediately."

On Speed vs. Governance (The "Bottleneck" Fear)

The Hard Question:

"Hub-and-Spoke models usually turn into the 'Department of No.' How do you ensure T-Tech doesn't become a bottleneck?"

The Answer:

"We don't force compliance; we incentivize it with speed. My deal with the business is simple:

  • Option A (Golden Path): Use our pre-certified blocks (e.g., the standard SAP Connector). Result: Auto-approval in 24 hours.
  • Option B (Custom): Build it yourself. Result: 4-week security review.

Most teams choose speed. This makes compliance the 'path of least resistance,' not a police action."

On Offshore Quality (The "German Quality" Fear)

The Hard Question:

"We need TÜV-grade quality. Can an offshore delivery model really deliver that, or will we spend all our time fixing their code?"

The Answer:

"Quality is a function of the Specification, not the location. In my model, 'Thinking' happens onshore (Munich) and 'Typing' happens offshore (Pune). We never send vague requirements to the delivery team. We send technical specs. Then, we enforce a mandatory Onshore Code Review before anything touches production. You get German engineering precision with offshore economics."

On Shadow IT (The "Divisional Autonomy" Fear)

The Hard Question:

"The divisions are powerful. If we are too slow or expensive, they will just build their own Shadow IT. How do you stop that?"

The Answer:

"We don't stop it; we out-compete it. Right now, if a Division builds their own app, they own the maintenance, the security patching, and the TISAX audit risk. Our value proposition is: 'Use our delivery model, and we indemnify you.' We take the maintenance and compliance headache away. That is a service they cannot build for themselves."
