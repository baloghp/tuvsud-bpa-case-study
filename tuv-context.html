The inflection point (TIC sector)

The Testing / Inspection / Certification industry is shifting from on-site expert delivery to data-driven, remote, and AI-supported assurance. For TÜV SÜD, that means becoming a digital trust partner without weakening the brand’s core promise: auditability and safety.

Aspirations (outside-in)
  • Become a digital trust partner by combining assurance heritage with digital delivery (data, remote, AI-enabled services). [2]
  • Prove “trust” in the next wave: AI quality, ethics, and regulation readiness as market differentiators. [4] [5]
  • Scale impact while staying credible on sustainability and compliance expectations. [1]
  • Maintain strong performance while evolving the portfolio (growth + modernization narrative). [3]

Implication for BPA: automation must increase speed and scale by making governance easier (evidence-by-default), not by adding manual gates.

Key tension
  • Speed (market + internal demand) vs compliance (audits, security, evidence).
  • Enable makers vs avoid chaos (duplication, shadow IT, uncontrolled AI spend).
  • German quality vs global scale (throughput, blended rate, time zones).

Why T-Tech exists (and why it matters)

TÜV SÜD Digital Service GmbH (“T-Tech”) is the group’s internal digital delivery arm: it provides digital services and software/automation delivery to the operating divisions under a service-provider model (with governance, pricing, KPIs, and accountability).

  • Mission (practical): accelerate digitalization while preserving TÜV-grade auditability (security/compliance by design).
  • Operating model: a separate GmbH delivering to internal customers; must show value, manage demand, and make delivery repeatable (factory economics).
  • Success definition: measurable business value (ROI/efficiency) + sustainable service delivery (pricing/chargeback) + governance that enables speed.

So what does this mean for BPA?

The BPA function is a business model + governance role as much as a technology role: define the automation service catalogue, enforce ALM/security standards, control spend (incl. AI), and demonstrate impact via KPIs/ROI — while keeping delivery fast.
